About Silent Library

A Sanctuary of Women's Words Since 1975

Welcome to Silent Library, a haven for knowledge, empowerment, and community—founded in 1975 with a bold vision: to preserve and celebrate the written works by women, for women.

Our Collection: A Living Archive

We house a vast and diverse collection of books, journals, and publications spanning:

  • Feminist theory & women's history
  • Literature & poetry by female authors
  • Practical guides (from housekeeping to self-sufficiency)
  • Rare manuscripts & grassroots zines

Whether you're a researcher, activist, or curious reader, our shelves hold wisdom waiting to be uncovered.

More Than Just Books—A Movement

We believe in amplifying women's voices beyond the page. That's why we host:

  • Author talks & book launches
  • Feminist discussions & workshops
  • Creative writing circles & storytelling nights
  • Research symposiums on gender studies

Why "Silent" Library?

The name reflects our origins—a quiet rebellion. In 1975, women's literature was often overlooked, so we created a space where these voices could speak loudly, even in silence.

"A library is not just a collection of books—it's a revolution on shelves."

Join Us

Whether you're here to study, share, or simply explore, you belong at Silent Library. Discover the words that shaped our past—and help us write the future.
